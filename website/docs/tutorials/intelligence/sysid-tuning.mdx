# SysId Tuning

Stop guessing PID constants. ARESLib features automated **System Identification (SysId)** for calculating perfect feedforward constants ($kS, kV, kA$).

:::important Championship Precision
To achieve perfect movement, you must understand the physics of your robot. SysId mathematically characterizes your motors, gears, and mechanism weight.
:::

## 1. What is SysId?

SysId is a tool developed by WPILib (and integrated into ARESLib) that runs specialized tests on your robot:
-   **Quasistatic Tests**: Slowly ramps up voltage to find the minimum power needed to overcome friction ($kS$) and the velocity constant ($kV$).
-   **Dynamic Tests**: Applies a step-voltage for a short burst to measure acceleration ($kA$).

## 2. Running a Test

ARESLib subsystems include built-in SysId routines. You can trigger them from the Driver Station or through our simulation environment.

```java
// Logic inside a Subsystem
public Command sysIdQuasistatic(SysIdRoutine.Direction direction) {
    return m_sysIdRoutine.quasistatic(direction);
}
```

### Procedure
1.  **Clear the Area**: Ensure the robot has at least 3 meters of open floor (for drivetrains) or full range of motion (for arms).
2.  **Deploy Code**: Flash the SysId-enabled OpMode.
3.  **Run Tests**: Execute the 4 tests (Forward/Reverse Quasistatic, Forward/Reverse Dynamic).
4.  **Export Log**: The `AresAutoLogger` will save a `.wpilog` file to your Control Hub.

## 3. Analyzing the Results

Open the **SysId Tool** on your laptop and load the `.wpilog` file. The tool will generate a "Best Fit" regression for your motor's behavior.

### Understanding the Constants
-   **$kS$ (Static Friction)**: The "deadband" voltage. The energy required to start moving.
-   **$kV$ (Velocity)**: The voltage required to sustain a specific speed. ($V = kV \times velocity$).
-   **$kA$ (Acceleration)**: The voltage required to change speed. ($V = kA \times acceleration$).

## 4. Applying the Constants

Once you have your constants, plug them into the ARESLib `Feedforward` classes:

```java
private final ElevatorFeedforward feedforward = 
    new ElevatorFeedforward(kS, kG, kV, kA);
```

By combining these physics constants with a minor PID loop, your robot will reach targets with absolute precision and zero overshoot.
